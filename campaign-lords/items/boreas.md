# Boreas

*+3 Icy Burst Cold Iron Bastard Sword*

### Forging and Features

This legendary blade was forged from the purest iron by sorcerous frost giants held captive by a great white wyrm. Upon completion, the dragon slew those who forged the blade. The greatest of the frost giant sorcerers bound himself into the blade in a desperate attempt to survive the slaughter and to exact revenge upon the dragon. While particularly ill-suited to slay white dragons, the blade desires to slay both dragons and those in authority. Although originally driven by desperation and a thirst for revenge, in the centuries since its forging Boreas' animating spirit has settled into a less frantic state. It does not rage or scream for blood. Instead, it has become as patient as only a large piece of iron can be, coolly waiting for opportunities to pursue its agenda.

Forged as a single piece of Cold Iron, the sword is of plain construction. The surface of the metal is dark and rough-hewn. No jewels or stones adorn the sword; its design is so simple as to be austere. When the long, tapered blade is drawn from its sheath, traceries of frost quickly spider across the surface.

Boreas was originally crafted with matching harness, but the belt was lost when Aidar Van Eck kicked the corpse of the previous wielder into a swollen mountain river. The belt was reputed to have significant enchantments of its own, including protective properties and spellcasting.

### Arrival and Acquisition

Boreas was born out of the icy northlands and into the Dragonvale by Yngve the Bastard. Yngve was a tall, muscular, blond barbarian from the far north, scion of a cruel land and a cruel bloodline. The legendary sword had been carried by his ancestors, to whom it was known as the Chief's Blade. Yngve, however, was not to be chief. As a younger, bastard son he would not inherit anything from his father other than his strength. Therefore, he stole the sword and killed his father and brothers in an ill-prepared bid to seize power. Driven from his home in defeat, he and his followers eventually turned to plain brigandry in the rich lands of the Dragonvale, preying on caravans and small towns. It was in fact a caravan raid that directly led to the destruction of the renegade barbarians.

Aidar Van Eck was one of the few survivors of one of Yngve's attacks, and he vowed revenge on everyone responsible for the loss of his steady pay. Joining with a party of adventurers, including many who would later rise to great power in the Vale, Aidar hunted the brigands for weeks. When finally cornered and defeated, Yngve fought his way out of the trap, killing several of the adventurers, and fled into the mountains. Aidar pursued him, and on the banks of a swollen mountain river, they fought. Yngve was older, stronger, and more skilled, in addition to wielding Boreas. Aidar, however, was in much better condition, not having fought his way out through a small army of enemies. After a long contest, Aidar landed the killing blow to Yngve, caving in his skull and causing him to drop the sword before his body tumbled into the rushing water.

When Boreas is drawn it always makes its influence felt. Usually this is limited to the circle of cold which encompasses the wielder. Sometimes, however, Boreas is more fully present. When fully unleashed the sword envelopes itself and its wielder in blue coldfire.

(AL CE; Int 10, Wis 12, Cha 12; Empathy, 30ft. vision and hearing; Ego score 12; Sense Motive +11.)*

The wielder and his equipment are always protected as though by the Endure Elements spell.

Each time the sword successfully deals cold damage, spikes of crystal ice sprout from the wound. Once 10 points of unhealed cold damage are dealt to a single opponent, that opponent is affected as though by the Slow spell for 1 round, Fort DC = 6 + cold damage dealt. The opponent must make a new save each round against the cumulative cold damage dealt. If the opponent suffers 5 or more points of fire/heat damage, or is magically healed for 5 or more hit points, the cold damage is reset. If the opponent takes a standard action to remove the ice crystals, the cold damage is reset.

1/day the wielder of Boreas can cast Polar Ray for 15d6 damage as a 15th level caster, range 60 ft. An additional use for that day is gained every time Boreas delivers the killing blow to a creature of 15 HD or greater.

1/week the wielder of Boreas can evoke it's frigid heart to empower the sword and wielder. The following effects last for 15 rounds: the sword becomes a +5 weapon, all of the sword's cold damage is automatically maximized, and the wielder is cloaked in a Chill Shield (see Fire Shield) at caster level 15 causing 1d6+15 cold damage to any melee attacker and halving fire damage to the wielder.

- *Diverging from the standard rules, wielders with alignments of NE, CE, and CN do not suffer a negative level for holding Boreas. Wielders of other alignments do suffer a negative level while holding Boreas. Boreas' alignment is not primary to its personality. Rather, its desire to slay dragons (good and evil alike) and those in authority is its primary drive.*
