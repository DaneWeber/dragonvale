# Present Plotlines

### Dragons

There are three dragons who the Order of Light report to be responsible for a major theft from the Hallstadt. The stolen treasure is some distance to the north and is guarded by the same three dragons responsible for the theft. The Order of Light has provided detailed information about the lairs and the dragons.

### Drow and Thel Kilsten

Thel Kilsten is a dwarf who has come to demand payment promised by the previous Lord Protector of Reichsfreyt. He is acting on behalf of the Black Widows, a group of Drow. His original statement was as follows:

"This certifies that Thel Kilsten, bearing the mark, is our agent. Our debtor, the Lord Protector of Reichsfreyt, is to entrust the first of the ten due payments to our agent by noon the day following Debtday, the first Warday of the month of Glittergold. This payment must be in gold equal in weight to 500 talents and must include the means of transportation which will either be returned or purchased. Our agent is authorized to engage in all necessary action on our behalf in order to secure that which is owed."

A "talent" of gold is 120 lbs. There are 50 Imperial standard coins to a pound. 10 payments of 500 talents at 120 lbs each, with 50 coins to a pound, is 30,000,000 gp total.

Upon examination of the contract, it is clear that it requires "even payments," and by pushing the point, it was agreed that repayment could begin with payment of each day's pro-rated amount of 41,096 gp. Jovrim borrow 100,000 gp from the city of King's Keep in order to make an initial payment of 82,192 gp to Thel Kilsten for the first two day's repayment.

Thel Kilsten was allowed to leave with the payment without being followed.

### Corpse-Trafficking

The red skull and the investigation of the excavations made clear that there was some sort of corpse-trafficking that was going on with the corpses from the Ironbar cemetery.

### Iron Golem & Old King

The iron golem near the corpse-trafficking excavation had markings clearly linking it to the old king's viscount, Karr Treckingstole.

### Jasidan Doings

The temple of Wee Jas is very protective and secretive, and is upset by the recent spying. That spying, however, revealed a considerable amount about their internal projects. While a library and odd dormitory were not terribly noteworthy, some sort of underground mechanical contraption that the Jasidans are studying was very noteworthy.

### Family of Tulipwink

Nellas Tulipwink was the victim of the iron golem. She was raised from the dead and returned to her family. The Tulipwink family seems very mistrustful and somewhat hostile toward the Lords Protector. Her business in the basement of Barret Poyn's furniture shop was never made clear.

### Ebondraught

A popular new drink in King's Keep is this dark syrupy beverage with a faint necromantic aura. It is being imported from the Empire of the Eternal Sun.

## Present Rumors

So, currently, there has been no PR. Rumors will quickly spread about the info available to onlookers.

Two cave-ins in the Ironbar cemetery occurred and there has been activity by the Lords in the locale. The Lords were known to be underground at the time of the second cave-in, with their new militia-men holding a perimeter. As to this, some people will assume that the Lords are saving the day again, which is what they always do, but others will be talking about the desecration of the Dragonvale's most well known graveyard which is trusted to be impervious to unholy activity. If they catch wind that the corpses were stolen or desecrated, they may be out for blood. Respect for one's ancestors is not a religion, but is still important enough that descendants usually feel a duty to avenge the desecration of their ancestor's remains. Ghostly hauntings are not completely unknown when remains have been disturbed.

The Lords were seen digging up a casket and opening it and removing an item from it, which they took with them. Some might take this to be "grave robbing."

Moving more slowly will likely be the rumors about the new Lord Reichsfreyt moving a heavily guarded cart from an inn to the King's Chamber. Jovrim is apparently taking something valuable into his possession.

The presence of a heavily-armored and scary-looking dwarf walking throughout the city, accompanied by Lord Unterwalden, is known by many, and certainly being talked about. Is this a new ally? Apparently someone attempted to kill the dwarf, and Lord Unterwalden protected the dwarf. This someone may well be sharing his beef with the dwarf in taverns.

Some will also be talking about the visits made by the Lords to Barret Poyn's furniture shop. Very odd, especially given its relative proximity to the Ironbar cemetery. He has also apparently been closed for business since the visits began, even though the Lords don't have jurisdiction over residents such as he.

The group of people making a visit to the King's Chamber may have been noted, and given their apparent dislike and/or distrust, the family may be spinning the matter against the Lords.

Oh, and the Jasidans may not be pleased that they were spied on in their temple, and now have a mess to clean up in the cemetery. The Jasidans do play a special role in protecting the cemetery.
