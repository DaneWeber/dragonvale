# I Wouldn't Have Worn These Boots

Lyrics (Best sung in an Irish accent)

As I went out one evening, it being an early moon,
I asked the gods of slaughter, "Grant unto me a boon.
Upon the men of Kell give me my vengeance just
And I will leave their widows' teeth gnashing in the dust."

Oh I butchered that man of Kell, I tore his balls out by the roots,
But if I'd have known he'd bleed so well, I wouldn't have worn these boots.
