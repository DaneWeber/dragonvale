# Aidar Van Eck

## Description

Aidar Van Eck is a barbarian from the distant south. He is a handsome man, with an aquiline face and dark, alert eyes. His dusky skin marks him as a foreigner in his current land, but the contrast with his close-cropped, not-quite-blond hair makes him truly exotic. Although possessing the great height typical of his kind, he is built on a leaner frame than his northern counterparts, with longs limbs of heavily corded muscle hanging from his broad shoulders. As is customary with his people, he usually wears a knee-length shendyt rather than the trousers more common in northern climes.

Van Eck is a Vendhyan, of the Aurochs clan. As a Lord Protector he has adopted the totem of his tribe as the crest of his house. Since the clans' identity is intimately tied to the land, the people of Vendhya only rarely leave their homelands, but Aidar abandoned them as a youth for love of a woman, or rather, its lack. When the girl he loved chose another for her husband, Aidar walked away from her, his tribe, and his people. He traveled far, never tarrying in one place, always pushing onward following rumors of treasure or adventure. He came to the Dragonvale as a mercenary in the service of a merchant, but after a disastrous ambush wiped out the caravan he was guarding along with his employer, he joined a company of independent adventurers to seek revenge, as well as the gold which was owed him.

Aidar is at home in his mountainous Canton of Unterwalden. He watches over his Canton from the fortress known as Curzack's Keep, where he lives with a strong garrison, a small staff, and his current mistress, the dancer Ilona. The Keep's immense walls were built centuries ago to deter invaders, and in the passing years those walls have only grown higher and thicker as generations of occupants have tried to outdo each other in adding to them. The Keep is set near the border with the Hordelands, high above the capital city of Blasingdell in the lower valley.

Having left his homelands as a youth, Aidar has roamed widely, and learned much of the world. The lesson he has taken most closely to heart is that all the treasure in the world is worthless without the strength to defend it, and enough strength can always get you more treasure. He has amassed a collection of weapons and combat magics, including the ice-enchanted bastard sword *Boreas*, in never ending preparation to win the next fight. When expecting trouble he wears a Mithril breastplate. The breastplate was taken as spoil, so Aidar does not know where it originated, but it is covered in richly detailed engravings of nautical images and sea creatures.

### Personality

Aidar could be broadly described as a paranoid sociopath, although this is not strictly accurate. A deeply tribal man, Aidar is utterly devoted to his friends, and equally callous to those he considers outsiders. Offering the world a wide smile and easy conversation, many think of him as open and friendly, but the smile is always surmounted by the eyes of a predator, and his banter never reveals his true intentions. Although quick with a comment or joke, he is slow to trust and rarely opens himself to anyone but his closest friends. In combat Aidar is willing to do whatever it takes to ensure victory. Usually this entails the direct application of overwhelming force, but he will not hesitate to employ deception and guile if he thinks they will improve his odds, and he has developed a reputation for cautiousness after several close calls early in his career. He believes in eliminating threats before they ripen, and tends to favor a scorched earth policy toward enemies. He never gives quarter, but will toss off a sarcastic quip to an opponent after death has ensured there will be no come-back.

Although a man accustomed to death, violence, and abominations, Aidar has retained that instinctive fear and hatred of undeath so often found in primitive people. While a man on the outside of civilization quickly becomes accustomed to death in its many forms, part of being familiar with the cycle of life and death is the *very* firm belief that dead things should stay dead. In his years of travel through many strange lands, including the Dragonvale, he has seen many things which ignored that principle, but Aidar is firmly of the opinion that messing about with things beyond death always leads to a bad end. Aidar, in fact, often *is* that bad end.
