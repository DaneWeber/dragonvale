# Altairan Mattiason

Chevalier (Knight) Altairan Mattiason, of the Order of Light. A Paladin sent to the Dragonvale to establish an outpost for his Order.

Altairan Mattiason is the son of the famous bard Mattias Dawnsong. Altairan was raised to believe that music and the telling of epic tales are the greatest vocations. Bards are the preservers of virtue who instill in the masses the best and greatest examples of goodness, bravery, and self-sacrifice. Without bards, Mattias was wont to say, most of the sentient races would never know what goes on beyond the borders of their tiny villages. For that reason, Altairan grew up believing that the best way to repay the greatest adventurers was to enshrine their deeds in song and story. Altairan's mother, Curonas, was always Mattias's most loyal devotee. In addition, Curonas often participated in Mattias's bardic performances, dancing with almost Sylvan grace and mischief to the music he played.

As Altairan entered his teenage years, however, he began to desire adventure and gallantry akin to the very stories his father told. Rather than *tell* the stories, Altairan wanted to be a *part* of them. When Altairan asked his father to let him train with a warrior or soldier, Mattias sternly refused. "Today there are already too many who focus on killing and war. Music and other such arts are cast aside as worthless hobbies with no place in 'the real world.' Bards have the most important calling of any, my son - we help the sentient races to make *sense* of the madness of war. Why should people go on slaughtering one another if there is no meaning to it? Heal the hearts of the peoples so that they continue to have the strength and resolve to fight." Altairan respected the will of his father and vowed to use his strength and inborn duende to help and heal the masses.

Altairan learned all he knows of chivalrous love from Mattias's treatment of Curonas. While Curonas assisted in Mattias's act on occasion, her primary role in the family was as the devoted wife and mother. Mattias loved his wife dearly and composed ballads, prose, and poetry in her honor, singing them fondly while on his travels. From Curonas, Altairan learned the art of healing, for Curonas was selfless in her devotion to the poor and the sick. On certain days, Curonas pulled Altairan from his father's side and brought him into the gutters and side-roads of the cities in order to seek out the most helpless peoples. Even today, in Altairan's home city, Curonas can be found devoting her time to these goals. Whenever he can, Altairan lends his time and strength to helping his mother in these simple acts of kindness.

Two months after Altairan's fifteenth birthday, he and his father left Curonas at home and set off for a city far to the East. The city lay in a war-torn fiefdom that needed all of the cheer it could get. On the road through the wasteland left by the war, Altairan and Mattias were waylaid by a group of gnomish refugees seeking aid. The refugees told them that their village had been overrun by marauders who were rapaciously victimizing the villagers. Mattias agreed to help through the power of his music, being a powerful bard at the time. The gnomes led the two men into the nearby hills and eventually to the mouth of a cave. When Mattias confronted the refugees about the situation, they fled down the hill screaming. Before either Altairan or Mattias was able to act, an ancient black dragon emerged from the cave and attacked. Mattias held the dragon's attacks and cried for his son to flee as far and as fast as possible. Altairan ran down the hill after the gnomish villagers, taking glances over his shoulder at his father. Mattias fought valiantly against the evil dragon, trying to use his songs to distract the dragon or lull it into sleep. Alas, in the end, the magic of even the mightiest bard often cannot overcome the destructive evil of a powerful dragon. Mattias, having seen very little combat and avoiding it as much as possible, was killed, his personal treasures added to the dragon's hoard.

The dragon gave chase after Altairan once Mattias was slain - it knew that he would make an easy kill. Spying Altairan from afar, the dragon swooped from the sky to destroy him with its acidic breath. It was at that moment that Altairan looked up to face his death, only to see a magnificent point of light drive into the dragon's side. Suddenly, three warriors on powerful steeds raced up the hill toward the spot beneath the hovering dragon. The light in the sky drove the dragon to the earth, putting it within striking distance of the trio on the ground. After a moment, Altairan was able to see that the light in the sky was actually a shining paladin mounted on a flying creature. The battle between the dragon and the four paladins lasted for only a few minutes, and the dragon ultimately died - its body burned by the paladins. When they had assisted Altairan with the burial of his father, the paladins offered to escort him wherever he wished to go. Having no other family, however, Altairan asked to simply be brought to the nearest city to find work. The paladins countered with an offer to join their ranks and fight against the darkness of evil as they did. Altairan hesitated at first, remembering his vow to his father - always to use his strength and abilities to heal and help. It was then that he realized that the paladins were an incredible mixture of strength for battle and the healing of evils. Altairan joined the Order immediately and rose quickly in prowess.

After the loss of his father, Altairan developed an understandable hatred of dragons. This is primarily a hatred of evil dragons, but he dislikes all dragons in general. For obvious reasons, he also hates gnomes for the part they played in his father's death. Altairan views all gnomes with suspicion and almost never trusts anything a gnome says.

Throughout his life, Altairan has possessed a personal magnetism that has helped him immensely. Many who knew his parents have remarked that Altairan inherited the best traits from both. While Altairan has sought tirelessly to crush evil wherever it is found, he considers it a far greater victory to convert and purify the wicked. Many people throughout his travels have tried to become followers to Altairan, he always dismisses them. As with any member of the Order of Light, Altairan shuns anything that might lead to political power or influence. "Far better for you to follow Heironeous or the ways of goodness from whence I *have* my power," Altairan tells these people.
