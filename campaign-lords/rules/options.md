# Options

This is a record of optional and custom elements known to exist in the Dragonvale world.

## Rituals

Custom rituals exist in the game world. They are mainly aimed at plot elements and to explain particular magical phenomena. No particular rituals have been recorded in the game, but your characters may have already dealt with rituals in their past. See the SRD for details: http://www.d20srd.org/srd/variant/magic/incantations.htm

## Alchemy

I like alchemy. Alchemy definitely goes beyond what is detailed in the SRD. I will mostly just have items from expansion books, but I may have other alchemical materials and interactions show up in the game.

## Ancient Structures

Certain ancient structures will have special properties without a clear method of construction according to normal rules. These might be a matter of Incantations and Alchemy, but they don't have to. It might just be a Craft Structure feat, but the point is that structures like the King's Chamber have special properties that go there by special means.

## Empyreal Law

This is something I have messed with in previous games and am including here. It is essentially science, and is entirely axiomatic. The idea is that certain fundamental structures of cosmic reality are governed by unbreakable laws, and that one can arrange special circumstances and structures that make use of these. This does not operate via magic, and thus is unaffected by an anti-magic sphere. It also isn't alchemy, but might be considered a brother to alchemy in the way that physics is a brother to chemistry. The most common instance of such things are the contracts that the scribes of the Empire of the Full Moon create. Empyreal law is also how Inevitables know to come enforce something. Empyreal law also is probably involved in the rules about the planes and the fate of souls.

## Custom Monsters

I reserve the right to make up completely original monsters. As it becomes important, I will note any custom monsters you have either learned about or dealt with. I will also customize monsters. My dragons, for example, will likely have a custom spell selection, skill selection, and feat selection, including options from the Draconomicon or other sources.

## Custom Magic Items

I have already created some custom magic item abilities, such as the "Monk's" feature on Jovrim's kama, which admittedly is based on the ability of an item in the Magic Item Compendium. Black Stag had custom displacement armor. I certainly plan to have custom magic items used by NPCs and monsters.
