# Rulings

This is a record of rulings and rules clarifications made by the DM. See the Options page for optional elements included in the game.

## Moving

**Moving Silently at Half Speed:** Use this modifier if the creature is moving at all and if the distance it moved during any single action during its last turn was equal to or less than half its current speed. If surface conditions limit its movement, use this modifier only if the creature moves no more than half the distance in speed that the conditions allow. For example, if the creature has a speed of 40, it's moving half speed if it moves 20 feet or less during any single action. If the creature is moving over terrain that hampers its movement, however, it can move only 20 feet with a single action and is considered moving at half speed only if it moves 10 feet or less during one of those actions. (From: http://www.wizards.com/default.asp?x=dnd/rg/20040921a)

## Talking

Unless a pause is specifically needed or important, it won't take time. Talking will. Six seconds of conversation will be a round. As a rule of thumb (don't abuse this by using obtuse but dense vocabulary), every 25 words of conversation by anybody not talking over somebody else will indicate a new round has gone by. Also, as a new general rule, you can only communicate with 10 words on your turn during a normal combat situation.

## Illusions

Please see the articles by Wizards of the Coast for clarification on illusions: Parts [One](http://www.wizards.com/default.asp?x=dnd/rg/20060207a), [Two](http://www.wizards.com/default.asp?x=dnd/rg/20060214a), [Three](http://www.wizards.com/default.asp?x=dnd/rg/20060221a), and [Four](http://www.wizards.com/default.asp?x=dnd/rg/20060228a).

### Invisibility and other Glamers

The subject of a glamer is not adversely affected by the glamer. He can see himself and his equipment. He is aware of the glamer, just as one can be aware of any illusion that is disbelieved.

### Invisibility and the movement of foes

When a creature chooses to move into a square containing an invisible creature, the invisible creature has the option to either step aside and allow the invading creature to pass without detection, or can prevent passage as normal. If the invisible creature prevents passage, the invading creature becomes aware of the invisible creature in the square.

### Invisibility and grappling

Once a grapple is engaged, invisibility and other forms of concealment do not apply to attack and grapple rolls among those engaged. If there is no clear way to discern an opponent (tactile or vision), and there are three or more creatures engaged in the grapple, then choosing an opponent for an action (such as pinning or causing damage) is random. Even so, there is no penalty to the rolls even with random opponent selection.

### Minor Image and other Figments

When a creature with an Intelligence of 3 or greater successfully strikes a figment, that constitutes proof that the figment is an illusion, and the creature automatically disbelieves. If any creature misses a figment, that still counts as an interaction, and allows a save to disbelieve.

## Grapple

### Options

Monks can use their [Flurry of Blows](http://www.d20srd.org/srd/classes/monk.htm#flurryofBlows) for grapple attacks in the grapple. When grappling, the Trip (no touch attack required), Disarm, and Sunder attacks are acceptable options when using the "Attack your opponent" choice. Of note, those grappling do not threaten any squares, so no attacks of opportunity are provoked against the grappled opponent.

### Prone

Becoming prone is not automatic at any point in the grapple. You can force all grappling combatants prone as part of a won "Move" action. While being "prone" is a separate status from being "grappled," being prone does not affect grapple checks, but does affect melee and ranged attacks.

### "Not Grappling"

Anyone involved in a grapple can choose to take a -20 penalty on all grapple checks with particular opponents in order to not be considered grappling those opponents. This is not an option if the opponent initiated the present grapple, or has succeeded at any of the following actions: damage your opponent, move, pin your opponent, break another's pin, or use opponents weapon.

### Throwing

You can move your opponent within your reach (own and adjacent squares for most medium creatures) without similarly moving if you are not considered grappling, or if you "release" your opponent as a free action as part of the "Move" action with which you move your opponent. You can force a release if you are pinning your opponent. If your opponent refuses to be released (choosing to similarly release you), you make your roll at -4, but force the release upon success.

## Magic

### Detect Magic/Evil/Undead/Etc.

After changing the precise area covered by the detection cone, the caster has to choose whether to treat the new cone area as a new detection, or as a continuation of the previous round's detection. If a continuation, then the caster concentrates and gets 2nd round information (number of auras and strength of the highest) about the auras that remained within the cone area. Any new aura caught by the new cone is completely ignored, and information about any aura that moved out of the cone is also ignored, even if it moves back within on subsequent rounds. Going from 2nd to 3rd round information works the same (strength and location of each aura, etc.).

All Detect spells operate simultaneously on both the Ethereal and the Material planes, regardless of which plane the spell is cast from. The normal rules for seeing an object or creature in order to match it to an aura remains.

### Clear Evidence of Magic

Beyond the verbal, somatic, material, and focus components of spell-casting giving away that something magic is happening, magic effects will, as a rule, be clearly extraordinary. That is, when a ward protects one from the warded attack, there is some display of the magical energy at play. Exceptions include the obvious one of illusions. Furthermore, mental-only effects also do not have a clear display. Mental-only effects include telepathy, charming, and detection. While there is not a list yet, don't assume that your magic will be unobservable until that is made clear.

### Magical Spell Descriptions

The names of spells in the published books are not the names as described in Common. When using Draconic, a spell can be described in just a few words, if not a single word. Draconic is well-developed for speaking about magic, and so when using that language, spells may be discussed with the frankness of D&D players. When using another tongue, however, the spells must be described, or else the Draconic term is used. So, mages generally intersperse their normal conversation with Draconic terms. Draconic is equally facile with all spells, Arcane and Divine. Druidic has precise terms for Druid and Ranger spells only. Celestial and Infernal have precise terms for all Cleric and Paladin spells.

### Divination Clarifications

Running water must be clearly running from one place to another. Rivers, streams, and aqueducts qualify. Lakes, swamps, and oceans do not.

For the Locate Creature spell, the three categories mentioned are such that "a certain type" is the most broad, such as "humanoids" or "shadow plane creatures." The "specific kind" category will generally correlate with a race that would have a Monster Manual entry, such as "Gnomes," "Gelatinous Cubes," or "Black Dragon." The "specific creature" category refers to a single, unique individual. For this, you must have seen the individual in question. For the "specific kind," you must have seen one member of that kind in the form of the one to be detected. If a rough sketch would only cover one of the forms, then you can only locate that form. For example, you can either locate butterflies or caterpillars, but not both, because their forms are too different even though they are the sames species.

Spells, such as Locate Create, that are fooled by the Polymorph spell, are also fooled by other transmutation magic that significantly alters a creature's anatomy, such as Shapechange, Human Form, and Alter Self.
