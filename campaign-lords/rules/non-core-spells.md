# Non-Core Spells

This page lists non-core spells known to exist in the world of Dragonvale. Please note that the DM may freely include additional spells for use by NPCs. These spells will be considered unusual unless listed here. Spells listed here should still be assumed to be uncommon and requiring research unless otherwise indicated.

**Precious Alchemy (Dane)**
Transmutation
Level: Sor/Wiz 0
Components: V, S, M
Casting Time: 1 minute
Range: Touch
Target: Object
Duration: Instantaneous
Saving Throw: None
Spell Resistance: No

This spell is the basis of the 10:1 ratio of precious coinage. With a single casting of this spell, you transform a quantity of copper, silver, gold, or platinum into a different one of equal value. Given the order of copper, silver, gold, and platinum, you may transform the starting quantity into 1/10th the weight of the next more valuable metal, or you may transform the starting quantity into 10 times the weight of the next less valuable metal. For example, you can transform 100 gold coins (weighing 2 lbs.) into 1,000 silver coins (weighing 20 lbs.) or into 10 platinum coins (weighing 1/5th of a lb.), but you cannot transform the gold coins into copper coins.

The product of this spell is in the general form as the starting quantity. So coins are transmuted into coins, dust into dust, and bars into bars. Magic items are immune to this spell.

Material Component: Up to 10 lbs/level of non-magical copper, silver, gold, or platinum.

**Human Form (Dane)**
Transmutation
Level: Sor/Wiz 3
Components: V, S
Casting Time: 1 round
Range: Personal
Target: You
Duration: 24 hours (D)
Saving Throw: None
Spell Resistance: No

This spell transforms you into a humanoid form. Humanoid creatures are unaffected by this spell, unless they are presently polymorphed, in which case this spell dispels the polymorph.

You become a humanoid of medium size. You assume a unique appearance determined by your nature, and you assume the same appearance every time you use this spell. You must use other means to disguise yourself if you do not want to be recognized by someone familiar with your Human Form appearance. Your appearance gives clues to your true nature, including skin, eye, and hair color that may not be normally possible for humanoids. The DM determines your appearance and which race you appear to be (generally Human).

You do not gain any abilities apart from those essential to a humanoid form (such as the ability to walk and hold items in your hands if you are normally a snake). If your natural form's species cannot see, hear, smell, taste, touch, or speak, you gain speech or whichever of these senses your species does not have. You do not regain speech or any sense if it was lost or impaired, regardless of reason. If your Human Form happens to look like a race other than Human, you do not gain any of that race's benefits.

You retain your ability scores with the following exception: your Strength and Dexterity are effectively lowered to be equal to 10 + your modifier. This essentially halves your modifier for Strength and Dexterity. This also benefits particularly weak or clumsy creatures. This has no affect on ability drain, damage, or enhancements.

You lose your Extraordinary Special Attacks while the spell is in effect. You lose your natural attack forms (such as Bite and Claw). Senses and Special Qualities that would plainly contradict a relatively normal, if cosmetically unnatural, humanoid appearance are suppressed. Suppressed senses and Special Qualities do not function (such as a Gelatinous Cube's Transparency, a Magmin's Melt Weapon's trait, or even a bat's echolocation-based Blindsense). You may use a swift action on your turn to unsuppress your suppressed senses and Special Qualities, but they remain unsupressed until the end of the spell.
