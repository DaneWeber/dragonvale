# Manors

Descriptions of the manors of the Lord Protectors of Hallstadt.

## Curzack's Keep

Manor of Unterwalden

Aidar Van Eck watches over his Canton from the fortress known as Curzack's Keep, where he lives with a strong garrison, a small staff, and his current mistress, the dancer Ilona. The Keep is set near the border with the Hordelands, perched on an outcropping high above the lower valley. It can only be approached by a steep road, winding under the watchful towers above. It was built as a fortress, not a palace, and only became the Lord's seat when Aidar took the title Lord Unterwalden. The Keep's immense walls were built centuries ago to deter invaders, and in the passing years those walls have only grown higher and thicker as generations of occupants have tried to outdo each other in adding to them.

Begun by Curzack, second Lord Unterwalden, as a sign of strength to the barbarian hordes which threatened his territory, the Keep is a classic stronghold. It incorporates layers of defense, starting with its location and including massive walls, towers, engines of war, and magical defenses built up over centuries of cautious improvement. Since the Keep is not a city and houses no civilian population, its wells and storehouses allow it to easily survive extended sieges.

## King's Keep

Manor of Reichsfreyt

This manor is a tremendous resource to the rulers of Hallstadt. In particular, King's Chamber is an extraordinary room in which to conduct political business. Through extraordinary construction, this large hall has an everwood tree growing from its roof and has the roots of the tree completely integrated into the ceiling, walls, and deep below the floor of the chamber. This nearly indestructible living thing prevents etherial and incorporeal entry. The doors even integrate the roots (which so far remain small and flexible). Something else about the construction, which involves an arcane pattern of gold and gem work within the walls and doors, protects the room against any sort of scrying or teleportation either in or out. The chamber is austere in its lack of furnishings, but is well decorated with tiles and mosaics depicting both decorative motifs and depictions of dragons being defeated by an assortment of heroic creatures, presumably the founders of Hallstadt. The central focus of the chamber is a large stone throne that has been estimated to weigh several tons.

The entirely above-ground fortress has many rooms and amenities for guests, enough to host 100 guests comfortably. These guest accouterments face inward on the manor's courtyard. Facing outward and looking down upon the city of King's Keep are fortified arrow-slits that can be sealed shut from the inside. The upper ramparts are fortified against attack from above.

## Watcher's Tower

Manor of Helvetia

Watcher's Tower is a tall tower built on top of a perch near the western entrance to the Dragonvale. The rocky perch that the tower is built on rises one hundred and ten feet about the valley floor. Although built to keep an eye on Galind's Pass, the most accessible gap between the mountains that form Helvetia's border, it has since become a center of government as well. In times past, the entrance to the tower could only be reached by a steep climb up a narrow staircase carved into the perch. Recently, however, the local leadership added a horse power pulley elevator.

Although lower levels have been converted to lush offices and conference halls, the tower contains no modernized sleeping areas. Because of these conditions, the roughly twenty five townsmen who spend their days in the tower all depart in the evening. Several soldiers remain on watch at all times in order to spot an invading threat. Several of the upper levels, which were once home to some of the Dragonvale's greatest warriors, have remained unattended for years and were immediately closed off by Jovrim when he took over as keeper of the tower.

## Zircon

Manor of Jura

A fortified edifice suspended unnaturally in the air above the crystal waters of Crescent Lake, a small body of water fed by the famous mineral springs of the region. When Verdigriss is at work, the light of the Zircon's furnaces reflects upon the still waters of Crescent Lake, and the peasantry are afraid.
