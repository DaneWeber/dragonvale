# Cantonal Government

## Lord Protector

Each canton has one Lord Protector. The Lord Protector is primarily charged with defending the canton from outside forces. This does, however, include out-of-town travelers (read: adventurers) who commit crimes. The Lord Protector may call up the militia and hire mercenaries. He is expected to engage in diplomacy for the canton, and meets with the other cantonal lords in regular Chamber meetings to discuss the safety of the valley. All cantons are bound together in the Hallstadt Confederacy and ensure mutual defense.

## Viscount

The Lord Protector appoints a viscount (the "vice") who has only whatever power the lord delegates. That said, either the lord or the vice must be within the canton at all times, and if the lord is absent the vice has full authority until the lord returns. The vice becomes the next Lord Protector upon the death of the first. That said, the canton's Consulate (essentially the legislative body) may at any time elect a new Lord Protector with a unanimous vote. While a vice may retain the lordship, the death of a lord is generally a good opportunity to select a new LP.

## Consuls

Consuls are called upon to resolve disputes, but everyone can appeal to the judges of Santh Cuthbert (and all criminal trials go to them). So a Consul's judgment aims to make both parties happy. Each canton has a different number of Consuls. While the Consuls set policy and law and such, the canton residents have the power to change any law with a popular vote, and popular votes may not be contradicted by the Consulate for 20 years. Consuls are elected by the people and serve until someone calls for a new election. They also gather every once in a while (when a canton calls for it) for the Grand Chamber to review the Confederacy's laws.

## Sheriff

The canton's sheriff works under the Consulate and is considered part of the judicial branch. He is charged with keeping prisoners and enforcing criminal sentences. The sheriff can call upon any nearby able-bodied individual to aid in the arrest of a violent criminal and can also deputize a posse beforehand. A criminal is generally apprehended by any able-bodied man in response to the hue and cry. Suits may be brought against those who fail to respond to the hue and cry.

## Mayor

Cities have mayors, who are in charge of guards, watchmen, and the ditchers, and manage the collection of city taxes. The mayors maintain the peace within the city, and he and his underlings perform the duties of the sheriff within the city, although not to the exclusion of the canton's sheriff.

## Captain of the Guard

The Captain of the Guard, appointed by the mayor, trains, arms, and maintains the city's guards. City guards watch over the gates to the city, inspecting traffic, and also patrol the city during the day. The look to prevent plague from entering the city, and to mark suspicious individuals with dangerous possessions. They look to maintain the peace, and have the authority to arrest those suspected of crime. They are also the standing military for guarding the city's walls in case of an attack or siege. While directly under the authority of the mayor, the captain of the guard and his men are required to carry out the lawful directives of the Lord Protector within the city limits. The canton's sheriff may also call upon the guard within the city limits.

## Captain of the Watch

The Captain of the Watch, also appointed by the mayor, trains, arms, and maintains the city's night watchmen. They patrol at night, and are largely the night-time equivalent of the guard, except that they are more lightly armed and generally considered more capable of running down a sneak. Watchmen are given the task of following suspicious people, investigating unusual situations, and protecting people deemed in danger. The watchmen include investigators who aid in solving crimes. They are also the de facto pub patrol, as most drunken altercations occur after sundown. The watchmen may be called upon by the chief porter to accompany ditchers engaged in dangerous work.

## Chief Porter

The Chief Porter is appointed by the mayor and is charged with maintaining public property. He employs a staff of "ditchers" who see to the maintenance of the city walls, gates, streets, ditches, sewers, and the canton's rural roads (an odd arrangement, seeing as they are outside of the city's jurisdiction). The ditchers also collect taxes and enforce building codes.
