# Unique Valley Locations

**Swampwood** - Large forested swamp nearby to King's Keep to the west. Largely left alone by the rest of Hallstadt. Halflings make up the major civilized presence in the Swampwood.

**King's House** - The site involves a number of stalls and semi-permanent shops on what was once probably a lawn. The main pathways feed around to the piers to which boats are moored. This location acts as a marketplace for trading between communities out in swamps and the wider dry dwellers. Fish make up a primary commodity being sold by the swamp-dwellers. The fresh fish for the day's meals are brought to the city early in the morning by a few fishmongers. The midday catches are sold to a few fish processors for preservation.

Dominating this scene is King's House itself. It is a three-story manor house that has seen better days. When the property was left without an heir, it became a commons and has seen the abuse incumbent on such a state. Squatters since claimed various rooms and maintained or neglected their sections to varying degrees. A large section of the manor has been consolidated into a single room-letting enterprise, but various other businesses and dwellings fill different rooms. A tavern has been set up out of the old kitchen and sees its best business when local denizens are driven indoors by inclement weather.

Hanging above the grand entrance to the manor, at least 12 feet above the steps, is a large iron barrel, the size of one of the big tun barrels at the jollier taverns. The rust-covered barrel is suspended by hefty bronze chains. The aesthetic value of such a decoration is questionable, but it is at least a prominent and recognizable identifier for the locale.
