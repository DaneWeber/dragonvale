# Hallstadt Law

The laws governing the Hallstadt are often simply understood by the residents, and are not necessarily codified. While the cities often have a number of regulations, the majority of the work of the law is taken up with settling disputes between people. These disputes are under the purview of the court that sees the case, and are more bound by tradition and "common sense" than by legislative documents.

Traditionally, however, it is understood that harm can be done in a number of categories: harm to person (includes death, physical injury, drugging, and pain), harm to possession (theft, vandalism, business contract violation), and harm to home (trespass, preventing public access to another's property).
